About

As defined by the World Health Organization (WHO), Mental health is a state of well-being in which a person realizes his or her own abilities, can cope with “life” stresses, can work productively and productively, and is able to contribute to the development of his or her community. Also, according to the WHO, mental health includes "subjective well-being, perceived self-realization, autonomy, competence, intergenerational dependence, and the self-realization of one's intellectual and emotional potential."

As a result of the 4th scientific and technological revolution, or information revolution, the percentage of people involved in information technology, “IT specialists”, has increased. Due to the peculiarities of some countries, "IT specialists" have filled the empty niche of the middle class, that is, they have become a full-fledged stratum in the country. The specifics of the work create additional mental problems. Therefore, in recent years, much attention has been paid to the importance of mental health in the technology sector. There is growing recognition of how pervasive mental health problems are in our "on-going" world. Employers are paying more attention to providing the necessary support mechanisms and creating an open environment in which stigma is removed when discussing mental health issues.

Our algorithm was successfully trained and showed excellent results on an independent test sample. And now we give you the opportunity to try out our system.
